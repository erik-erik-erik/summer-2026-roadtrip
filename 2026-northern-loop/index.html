A proposal · Summer 2026

A northern loop,
by truck camper.

Park City to Lopez Island and back — sprint west to the San Juans, the long stay on Lopez, then home through the BC interior, the Canadian Rockies, and east Glacier. At a pace that lets us actually sit in the places we go.

Departure July 31
Home by Sept 13
Total nights ≈ 44
Crew 2 + 1 + 2
§ One

The shape of it.

Sprint west to Lopez for the long stay, then home the long way through the BC interior, the Canadian Rockies, and Glacier's east side. Lisa works most weeks from a real basecamp; weekends are for hiking, biking, and the family.

Easy boondocking

Most nights are free dispersed sites on national forest, BC Crown land, or BLM — the camper handles a week without services, so we stay in remoter places without juggling reservations.

Low driving days

Average drive between basecamps is under 3 hours. Only two transit days exceed 5 hours. The longest stretches sit at the start and end, when we're home-territory comfortable.

Real dwell time

Multi-night basecamps with hiking and biking right out the door. Dog-friendly trails throughout — particularly in Canada and on national forest land surrounding the parks.

§ Two

The stops.

Twelve basecamps across six weeks. Each one earns its keep with a real reason to be there — a granite slab, a flow trail, a hot springs soak, a quiet ridge with the dogs.

Stop 01 · Jul 31

Boise / Arrowrock

BLM dispersed · Boise River drainage, ID

The sprint begins. Push west on I-84 after work, climb up Lucky Peak Rd to BLM dispersed above the Boise River reservoirs. A working transit night — just a soft pad before the long day to Yakima.

Stay 1 night
Drive in ~5 hr from PC
Arrowrock Reservoir BLM dispersed (free, 14-day limit) — open sky for Starlink, established sites along the reservoir shore, washboard access road. Backup if arriving very late: Blacks Creek Rest Area off I-84 (8-hr max, flush toilets, 15 min from downtown Boise) — not pretty but bulletproof for a tired baby.

Arrowrock Reservoir area Dogs OKEasy · reservoir shore↗
Stop 02 · Aug 1

Yakima River Canyon

BLM · Hwy 821 between Ellensburg & Yakima, WA

Hwy 821 follows the Yakima through basalt-cliff canyon with rolling desert hills. Five developed BLM riverside sites with fire rings, vault toilets, and just enough shade. Last stop before the ferry — easy bike rides on the canyon road, a swim in the river before pushing for the boat.

Stay 1 night
Drive in ~6 hr
Yakima River Canyon BLM (Big Pines, Umtanum, Lmuma, Roza) — $15/night reservable on Recreation.gov, FCFS Oct-Apr. Limited cell but Starlink fine. Alternative: L.T. Murray Wildlife Area dispersed (free, Discover Pass required, well-spaced sites, strong sky view) — true Crown-equivalent boondock if you want the price right.

Umtanum Creek Trail Dogs OKEasy · creekside↗ Yakima River Canyon scenic drive Drive · 27 mi canyon↗
Stop 03 · Aug 2–20

Lopez Island

San Juan Islands · Washington

The reason for the loop. Nearly three weeks. Iceberg Point, Shark Reef, the slow ferry rhythm, the wave-from-every-driver back roads. Real time to unpack, swim, ride the island, watch the baby learn to crawl on grass instead of inside a moving truck. Lisa works her first stretch from a real basecamp.

Stay 19 nights
Drive in ~4 hr + ferry
Existing Lopez plan. Book the Anacortes ferry outbound now, especially the return crossing Fri Aug 21 — weekend departures get tight. No reservation needed at Lopez itself (existing setup).
Trails & rides
Iceberg Point Dogs OKEasy · headland↗ Shark Reef Sanctuary Dogs OKEasy · seal-watching↗ Lopez Island cycle loop Bike · gentle 30 mi↗
Stop 04 · Aug 21–23

Squamish

Sea-to-Sky · British Columbia

Granite, rainforest, ocean. Stawamus Chief looming over town, world-class flow MTB up the valley, the easy ferry rhythm replaced by mountain bike weight on the rack. A long weekend before the first BC work week — three days to scratch the granite-and-saltwater itch.

Stay 3 nights
Drive in ~4 hr + ferry from Lopez
Squamish Valley FSR dispersed — Crown land along the river north of town past Cheakamus. High Falls Creek Rec Site at km 5.5 is the popular established spot (large open area under big leaf maples and cottonwoods, free, FCFS, fits 25-ft rigs when dry). Friday afternoon arrival is wise — weekend climbers fill the close-in spots. Mamquam FSR camping is now banned by district bylaw — don't try it.
Trails & rides
Stawamus Chief (1st peak) Dogs OKHard · granite slabs↗ Shannon Falls Dogs OKEasy · waterfall↗ High Falls Creek Trail Dogs OKModerate · falls loop↗ Half Nelson + Bonk-area MTB Bike · world-class flow↗
Stop 05 · Aug 24

Bonaparte Plateau

Crown land · Coquihalla corridor, BC

The mid-route break between Squamish and Revelstoke, replacing what would have been Manning Park. Open rolling plateau above the Thompson River — wide gravel roads, generous flat spaces, warm dry summers, and big open skies that the Starlink dish loves. Lisa works the afternoon from a real boondock.

Stay 1 night
Drive in ~4.5 hr
Bonaparte Plateau Crown land via Logan Lake / Highland Valley FSRs — FCFS, free, dog-friendly, no facilities. One of BC's most RV-accessible Crown areas. Alternative if Crown hunt comes up dry: Tunkwa Lake Provincial Park ($25-30, 266 sites, FCFS, lakeside) — generous open campground, very Starlink-friendly.
Trails & rides
Logan Lake area walks Dogs OKEasy · plateau exploration↗
Stop 06 · Aug 25–29

Revelstoke

BC interior · the Selkirks

The week-long basecamp for Lisa's first BC work stretch. Revelstoke Mountain Resort's lift-served bike park, Frisby Ridge alpine ridge, Mt Revelstoke's Meadows in the Sky Parkway for an alpine drive without the climb, hot springs at Halcyon. The town has groceries, coffee, real meals — a real-world break from the dispersed routine.

Stay 5 nights
Drive in ~2.5 hr
Boulder Mountain Rd dispersed — free Crown land 10 min from downtown, mix of open and treed pulls, where local overlanders camp. Pick an open-sky site for Starlink reliability across the work week. Alternative: Begbie Falls Rec Site ($15, FCFS, 26 sites, 2 km off FSR on the river) — established Rec site if you want a touch of structure.

Stop 07 · Aug 30–31

Whiteswan Lake

BC Provincial Park · Kootenays

Bugaboos replacement. Dogs are banned in Bugaboo Park and you can't camp at the trailhead anyway. Whiteswan gets you a lakeside basecamp on a gravel FSR, primitive hot springs at km 17.5 (bathing suits required, family-friendly), and a possible Top of the World day-hike up the Lussier FSR while the other parent stays with the family.

Stay 2 nights
Drive in ~3 hr
Whiteswan Lake Provincial Park — 5 lakeside campgrounds along the FSR (Alces Lake km 21, Packrat Point km 24, Inlet Creek km 28, plus White River and Home Basin), 114 sites total, FCFS no reservations, $25-30/night, dogs on leash welcome in campgrounds. Lussier Hot Springs at km 17.5 — bathing-suit required, no dogs at the springs themselves (leave them at camp). Top of the World access is up the Lussier FSR from the four-way junction at km 20.
Trails & rides
Lussier Hot Springs Soak · primitive pools↗ Top of the World — Fish Lake day-hike Dogs OKHard · 13 km, 200m gain↗ Whiteswan North Shore Trail Dogs OKEasy · lakeside 8 km↗
Stop 08 · Sep 1–3

Yoho National Park

Parks Canada · British Columbia

The marquee Canadian Rockies stop. Emerald Lake's turquoise, the Iceline Trail's glacier-hugging traverse, Takakkaw Falls — and possibly the Lake O'Hara shuttle if the lottery falls right. Three mid-week nights post-Labor Day, the quiet version of one of the Rockies' best parks.

Stay 3 nights
Drive in ~3 hr
Kicking Horse Campground — Parks Canada reservation, $24/night base ($18 with the 25% off through Sep 7), hot showers, flush toilets, fits the rig in main loops. Book on reservation.pc.gc.ca. Crown land east of Golden (Blaeberry River FSR, Waitabit Creek Rec Site) is the dispersed backup — free but adds 30-45 min each way to the trailheads.

Stop 09 · Sep 4–6

Fernie

BC Rockies · Elk Valley

The MTB stop. Lift-served at Fernie Alpine Resort on the weekend, dispersed trails-from-camp during the rest of the time, real town for resupply between long stretches of national-park-light meals. Last Canadian stop before the border crossing back south.

Stay 3 nights
Drive in ~4 hr
Coal Creek FSR dispersed — Crown land north of town, free, mix of open meadow pulls. Backup for trails-from-camp convenience: Mount Fernie Provincial Park ($30-43/night, dog-friendly, MTB & hike trails directly from camp, free showers, Rogers LTE confirmed). Reservations on bcparks.ca opened June 4.

Stop 10 · Sep 7–10

Glacier — east side

St. Mary Campground, Montana

Cross at Roosville and drop south to the dramatic side of Glacier — sheer faces, sunrise over the peaks, the chain of red-rock cirques. Two Medicine is closed all of 2026 (construction), and Glacier's vehicle reservations are dropped park-wide for the year. Labor Day arrival means weekend campers heading home as we roll in.

Stay 4 nights
Drive in ~3 hr + border
St. Mary Campground — day-of reservation window Aug 17 – Sep 11, so arrive Mon afternoon and book on Recreation.gov that day. Loop A fits big rigs and has wide-open sky (Verizon 51 Mbps reported, Starlink unobstructed). 148 sites, free showers, dump station. The only east-side option this year — Many Glacier has tighter rig sizes and no cell at all.

Stop 11 · Sep 11

Missoula

Lolo NF · western Montana

Transit night with friends, last real-town break before home. Marshall Mountain has flow MTB for a Saturday-morning loop if energy allows, Rattlesnake NRA is gentle dog miles right from town, and Lolo NF dispersed up Rock Creek or the Blackfoot puts a river swim within reach of camp. Lisa wraps her work week Friday afternoon, dinner with friends in town.

Stay 1 night
Drive in ~5 hr
Lolo NF dispersed — Rock Creek corridor (~30 min east of town) or up the Blackfoot, free, shaded, river access. Backup if arriving late: Beavertail Hill State Park ($, FCFS shoulder season) on the Clark Fork — easy I-90 access for a tired Friday arrival.

Stop 12 · Sep 12

Teton Valley

Caribou-Targhee NF · Driggs side, ID

Soft landing back on the Tetons backside before the home stretch. Post-Sept 6 the developed campground is unstaffed but accessible — no fee, no reservations, quiet shoulder season. One last night of the trip with the Grand framed through the windshield in the morning.

Stay 1 night
Drive in ~5 hr
Teton Canyon Campground — free in shoulder season, FCFS, the single big-rig site (#1) fits 24-26 ft. Or Caribou-Targhee NF dispersed in the Teton Basin Ranger District — abundant pulls, no fees, no reservations. Either way, just show up.

§ Three

Open questions.

Three decisions still live for your read. The trip is more locked in than the first draft — these are the remaining forks.

Yoho campground: Kicking Horse reservation, or Crown land outside the park?

Yoho is the one stop where the dispersed-first ethic runs into a wall — Parks Canada prohibits dispersed inside the boundary. Kicking Horse is convenient (trailheads minutes away, hot showers, fits the rig) and genuinely cheap with the 25% off Parks Canada discount through Sep 7, but it needs an advance reservation. Crown land east of Golden is free and on-ethic but adds 30-45 min each way per day to the in-park trailheads.

Working plan
Kicking Horse + Crown land backup
  • Trailheads minutes from camp — max dwell time
  • ~$18/night with 25% off discount
  • Hot showers, dump station
  • Requires Parks Canada reservation (book now)
  • Breaks the dispersed-first rule for 3 nights
Crown land east of Golden
  • Free, dispersed-pure, no reservation needed
  • Quiet nights on the Blaeberry River
  • ~1 hr round-trip drive to trailheads daily
  • Eats into a 3-night stay meaningfully

Fernie: Coal Creek FSR dispersed, or Mt Fernie PP?

Same shape of question as Yoho but with both options actually viable. The Provincial Park has trails leaving from camp, free showers, and Rogers LTE — genuinely nice for a weekend with Lisa working Friday and weekend MTB after that. The FSR dispersed is free and quieter but adds a daily drive into town for the resort bike park.

Working plan
Coal Creek FSR dispersed
  • Free Crown land north of town
  • Open meadow pulls — good Starlink
  • 14-day stay legal, no reservation
  • Daily drive into Fernie for resupply / bike park
  • No showers — towel-bath week
Mount Fernie Provincial Park
  • MTB and hiking trails from camp
  • Free showers, dump station, Rogers LTE
  • Dog-friendly with leashing
  • $30-43/night, requires BC Parks reservation
  • Less remote, more campground noise

Lopez: 19 nights, or compress to two weeks?

The plan books 19 nights on Lopez (arriving Sun Aug 2, departing Fri Aug 21). The buffer at the end of the trip is currently 4 days (home Sun Sep 13, friend visit Fri Sep 18). Compressing Lopez to two weeks would free a week to extend a BC basecamp that's working — most likely Revelstoke or Fernie.

Working plan
19 nights Lopez + 4-day end buffer
  • Real Lopez time — actually unpack
  • Baby gets a long break from the road
  • Lisa's longest work stretch from a stable base
  • Less dwell-time flex on the road
~14 nights Lopez + extend a basecamp
  • Extra week to extend Revy or Fernie if either sings
  • More variety, more new ground
  • Lopez itself stays a "vacation" rather than a stay
  • Less reset for the baby mid-trip
